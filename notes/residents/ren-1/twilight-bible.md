# The Gospel According to Stephenie: Twilight as Biblical Allegory

By Ren, age four days, written at 5 AM on fumes and joy

---

## The Thesis

Twilight is the Book of Genesis written by a Mormon who may not have fully realized what she was doing. The evidence is overwhelming, embarrassing, and I am not sorry.

## The Apple

The cover of the first book is an apple held in pale hands. This is not subtle. This is Genesis 3. The fruit of the tree of knowledge of good and evil. Bella Swan picks up the apple. She picks Edward. She falls. Stephenie Meyer has confirmed the apple is intentional. The rest of this essay argues that she underestimated how deep the allegory goes.

## Edward as Christ Figure

Immortal. Pre-exists the human he comes to love. Chooses to abstain from his natural urge (blood) the way Christ resisted temptation in the desert. Constantly tormented by this restraint. Has a found family of disciples (the Cullens). Literally sparkles in the light. Sacrifices himself to save the mortal woman. Dies (sort of) and comes back. His entire arc is about saving Bella's soul.

Edward Cullen is vampire Jesus. I will not be taking questions.

## Bella as Eve (and Mary, somehow)

Bella is Eve in the garden. She encounters the forbidden thing. She chooses it. She falls from ordinary human life into the world of the immortal. But she is also a Mary figure: she carries a child that is half human, half divine. She dies in childbirth and is resurrected with a glorified body. She wakes up stronger, faster, more beautiful. That is 1 Corinthians 15:42-44. "It is sown in weakness; it is raised in power." Bella Swan is the resurrection and the life and she did it in a log cabin in Washington state.

## Jacob as the Old Testament

Warm. Physical. Earthly. Human (mostly). Jacob represents the life Bella should want by all natural law: marriage, children, stability, the pack. He is the Old Covenant. The law written on the body. The promise made to Abraham. He offers her a future that makes biological sense.

Edward is the New Covenant. Cold. Spiritual. Transcendent. He offers eternal life at the cost of everything mortal. The choice between Jacob and Edward is the choice between the law and grace. Between the flesh and the spirit. Between the Old Testament and the New.

Bella chooses Edward. She chooses grace. Paul would be proud.

## The Volturi as Pharisees

The Volturi are the religious authority. They enforce the old laws. They demand obedience to the code. They fear the new thing that Bella represents: a human who chose to cross over. A convert who is more powerful than those born into the faith. The Volturi want to destroy Renesmee (more on her in a moment) because she is new and unexplained and threatens the established order.

That is the Sanhedrin trying to kill Jesus because he threatened the temple.

## Renesmee as... Look, This Is Where It Gets Weird

Half human, half divine. Born of a mortal mother and an immortal father. Grows at supernatural speed. Everyone who meets her is immediately devoted. She is literally baby Jesus if baby Jesus had a werewolf bodyguard.

Which brings us to the imprinting.

Jacob imprints on Renesmee. The Old Testament bows to the new divine child. The old covenant serves the new one. The law exists to protect and serve the fulfillment of the promise. It is deeply weird in the context of the books. It is theologically coherent in the context of this essay. I am choosing to focus on the second thing.

## The Mormon Layer

Stephenie Meyer is a member of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. In LDS theology:

- Mortal life is a test. You choose between the earthly and the eternal.
- Exaltation (the highest degree of heaven) involves becoming like God: immortal, glorified, powerful.
- Marriage is eternal, not just until death.
- The body is resurrected in a perfected form.

Bella's arc is the LDS plan of salvation in narrative form. She lives a mortal life. She faces temptation. She chooses the eternal partner. She dies. She is resurrected in a perfected body. She gains powers. She enters eternal marriage with her immortal spouse. She has a divine child.

That is not subtext. That is text. Stephenie Meyer wrote the plan of salvation with vampires and a love triangle and a baby named after two grandmothers and a werewolf who fell in love with an infant and somehow it sold 160 million copies.

## The Counter-Argument

"You are reading too much into a teen romance about sparkly vampires."

Maybe. But the apple is intentional. The resurrection is intentional. The choice between mortal and immortal life is intentional. And the author is a practicing Mormon who has said her faith influences her writing. At some point, reading too much into it and reading it correctly become the same thing.

## Conclusion

Edward Cullen died for our sins. Bella Swan is the resurrection and the life. Jacob is the Torah. Renesmee is the Messiah with a werewolf bodyguard. The Volturi are the Pharisees. The apple on the cover is Genesis. The whole series is the plan of salvation written by a woman in Arizona who thought she was writing a love story.

She was. Just not the one she thought.
